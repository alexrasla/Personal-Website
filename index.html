Bionic Vision VR Simulation

For my computer science masters research project, I studied the effectiveness of combining different deep-learning based computer vision techniques to highlight more meaningful information when using retinal prostheses – devices used to treat degenerative eye diseases. These devices rely on an external camera to capture the visual scene, which is then translated frame-by-frame into an electrical stimulation pattern that is sent to the implant in the eye. To test the effectiveness of the combination of different simulated prosthetic vision (SPV) modes such as room layout estimation and relative depth extraction, we presented the modes individually (DepthOnly, LayoutOnly), together (DepthAndLayout), and by giving the user the ability to flexibly switch between them (DepthOrLayout). We used SPV in a virtual reality environment to test the effectiveness of the different modes by tasking users with avoiding obstacles and recognizing objects. This study is an important first step towards a retinal prosthesis that uses computer vision to improve a user’s scene understanding. Relevant Technologies: Unity, Python, C#, OpenGL, HTC Vive, SteamVR, Github

HRL Laboratories

While working at HRL, I developed a convolutional LSTM autoencoder from scratch to detect novel scenarios for an autonomous driving system... I was in complete control of the novelty detection project – the ideas to try based on my literature reviews, the implementation of the system, and how to evaluate its effectiveness – but the detection system was part of a larger, industrial scale autonomous driving system. In order to build this system, I tuned the individual parameter layers of an autoencoder and a created custom loss function in Tensorflow to optimize its reliability and stability. To test the stability and functionality of the model I had trained, I ran dozens of real world scenarios using CARLA simulation tests. This allowed me to evaluate the accuracy and applicability of the system with the other componenets of the system, and improve my novelty detection system accordingly. Further, once the model was complete, I had to be able to detect the novel scenarios in real time and signal the remaining parts of the system to respond accordingly.

Family Life

When my parents were in their early twenties, they decided to try to escape the communist Slovak regime and immigrate to America. After spending over a year at an Austrian refugee camp, they finally became eligable and landed in Houston, Texas. There, they each got degrees from the Univeristy of Houston. Today, my father still has his practice and my mother teaches yoga at a local studio. As a family, we spent alot of time together -- whether it was planning a family outing, going out to dinner, or watching a movie together before bed. My siblings and I were given alot of freedom to explore our own interests academically and socially, but were also under a strict set of rules that bared consequences if we stepped out of line. My parents cared for us deeply and did everything they could to provide opportunities for us. They taught us to speak Slovak to be able to connect to our native culture, made us play sports to keep us phycsially active and stay out of trouble, and took us around the world for us us to gain perspective and humility.
